Glossary of Terms

LOCADTR

LOCADTR is a clinical tool, used in conjunction with a full assessment of an individual presenting for Substance Use Disorder treatment. The purpose of the LOCADTR is to determine the most appropriate recommended level of care based on the clinician's answers to the individual's risks and resources.

Bed Status OPEN

A currently available bed

Bed Status ON HOLD

An open bed for which a specific person is being assessed for admission. If that admission does not take place as expected, this can become an open bed.

Bed Type

These numeric classifications identify the OASAS licensed category of the bed. For detailed information regarding the specific levels of care, refer to OASAS Substance Use Disorder Service Descriptions.

Other Levels of Care

This web page includes only inpatient, detox and 820 residential resources. Many people are assisted in their recovery in less intensive settings. Refer to OASAS Substance Use Disorder Service Descriptions for detailed information about:

  • Outpatient Services
  • Opiod Treatment Services
  • Residential Services
  • Recovery Support and Housing Services
  • Prevention Services
Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) is the use of medications, in combination with counseling and behavioral therapies, to provide a whole-patient approach to the treatment of substance use disorders. Research shows that when treating substance use disorders, a combination of medication and behavioral therapies is most successful. It is not necessary to be in an inpatient or detox level of care to receive MAT.
